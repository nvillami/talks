Dimension of spline spaces

 Theorem:  For a simplicial complex $\Delta\subseteq \mathbb{R}^2$ \begin{align*} & {\scriptsize \dim S_d^r(\Delta) \leq \binom{d+2}{2}+f_1^0\binom{d+2-(r+1)}{2}-\sum_{i,\,\tilde{t}_i=1}^{f_0^0}\binom{d+2-(r+1)}{2}}\\[.5ex] &{\scriptsize -\sum_{i=1,\, \tilde{t}_i\geq 2}^{f_0^0}\biggl[\tilde{t}_i\binom{d+2-(r+1)}{2}\hspace{-0.1cm}-\tilde{b}_i\,\binom{d+2-\tilde{\Omega}_i}{2}-\tilde{a}_i\,\binom{d+2-(\tilde{\Omega}_i+1)}{2}\biggr]} \end{align*}   $\tilde{t}_i=$ number of edges connecting the vertex $\gamma_i$ to the boundary or vertices of lower index,
     ${\small \tilde\Omega_i=\bigr\lfloor\frac{\tilde{t}_i r}{\tilde{t}_i-1}\bigr\rfloor+1,\quad \tilde{a}_i=\tilde{t}_i (r+1) +(1-\tilde{t}_i)\tilde \Omega_i,}$  and   ${\small \tilde{b}_i=\tilde{t}_i-1-\tilde{a}_i.}$

Hierarchical box splines


Box splines have small support, are positive inside their support, and form a partition of unity.

They allow to define hierarchical spline spaces and the construction of spaces with adaptive local refinement.


Yvar et al. 2005.
